The Stuff School Didn't Teach You

You can probably solve for x ….but do you know how to read a payslip, get on the property ladder, or start investing without guessing? SSDT is the missing manual for adult life in the UK — money, renting, tax, employment rights, business, investing and more, taught in short, straight-talking modules. No fluff, no 40-hour course, no jargon.

Why this exists

School taught you Pythagoras and plenty of other things you'll never use again — and skipped the ones that actually shape your money, your home and your future. SSDT fills that gap: real UK situations, explained properly, in minutes, not hours.

What's inside

Nine areas of adult life, each broken into short modules you can finish in minutes, on your phone.

Money

Build credit that actually works for you, dodge debt traps like Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL), and save with a plan instead of good intentions.

Life Setup

Know what you're actually agreeing to before you sign a tenancy, set up energy, broadband and council tax without overpaying, and understand what it takes to buy your first place.

Work

Understand your payslip, know what you're legally owed, and choose the right route — apprenticeship, uni, or straight into work.

The Law

What you're actually entitled to, from tenancy rights to consumer protection to what happens if you're stopped by police.

Investing

ISAs vs savings accounts, how to actually start investing, and how property investing (buy-to-let, REITs) actually works.

Entrepreneur

Turn an idea into a real business: test it before you build it, register as a sole trader or limited company and sort your tax and bookkeeping, and land your first paying customer.

The Internet & AI

Protect your data, spot what's fake, and use AI tools without getting played by them.

Health

Recognise when money stress, burnout or bad sleep are quietly running your life, know how to navigate the NHS and mental health referrals, and learn how to actually ask for help — for yourself or someone you're worried about.

Decision Making

A way to think through the decisions that feel impossible — job offers, moves, big calls — so you can actually move.
